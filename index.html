2. Workplace Communications
2.1.0 Barriers to Effective Communication

Mental and physiological factors:

2.1.1 Hearing vs Listening

Hearing is a passive physiological process.
Listening is an active process that involves understanding and comprehending what is heard, which requires attention and engagement.

2.1.2 Active Listening

This is challenging when nonverbal things such as head nods are not an option, so we must do even more with our voice.

Paraphrase and repeat back
Ask clarifying questions
"okay, uh-huh, etc.." is better than nothing

3. Ethics
3.1.1 Why do people make unethical choices?

Not all unethical chioces are intentional. They are often made from "close enough" pattern matching that resulsts in drift from policy.

3.1.2 System 1 vs System 2

System 1 is responsible for the automatic reaction to a que or stimulus. System 2 is used when there is an intentional effort made to analyse the situation before reacting.

3.1.3 How to avoid making unethical choices

Make good habits so that System 1 responses will be ethical ones. This is extremely important as System 1 is responsible for 75-90% of behavior.

4. Behavioral Health Awareness
4.1.0 Psychotic Disorder

Schizophrenia

Suggested approaches:

Don't get involved in their delusions/hallucinations

If you suspect voices are present, ask:
Are you hearing voices?
What are they saying?
Have you ever acted on what the voices tell you?
How do you cope with hearing these voices?
What have you done in the past to resist them?

4.1.1 Mood Disorder

Bipolar Disorder

Suggested approaches:
Let them talk – if their speech is coherent and productive. If not, use redirecting techniques – bring them back to the topic.
Being a good listener is critical with the person with Bipolar Disorder. There will be “nuggets” of information in their words that may be helpful to guide next steps.

Clinical Depression

Suggested approaches:
Active listening
Ask directly about suicide.
Asking a person if they are thinking about suicide or killing themselves will NOT cause them to consider suicide when they are not considering it already.

4.1.2 Anxiety Disorder

Post Traumatic Stress Disorder

Suggested approaches:
Establish if the person is safe and reassure them you are there to help
Calm voice and slow speech down

4.1.3 Suicide

Be direct: "Are you thinking of killing yourself?" This will NOT cause a person to consider suicide as an option if they were not already there.
Do they have a plan?
Do they have the means?
